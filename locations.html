Reykjavik

Reykjavik, the Iceland capital, has a population of just over 200.000 people. It is one of the country's most vibrant cities. Beside it being Iceland's cultural and economic center it is also a popular tourist destination. It is believed that Reykjavik is Iceland’s first permanent settlement established in 1785. City is also one of the cleanest and safest cities in the world.

Vik

Located about 186 km from Iceland's main capital, Vik is a village like no other. With only 300 people living in the village. What makes Vik so visited by tourists is the nature that surrounds this village. Within the walking distance of the village you will find the famous black sand beach, “Reynisfjara”. Another famous tourist attraction found just outside Vik is Mýrdalsjökull Glacier, where tourists can visit and go on hikes. Some other attractions near Vik worth visiting are: Hálsanefshellir cave, Dyrhólaey and Ice Caves.

Akureyri

Akureyri, a town stationed in the northern part of Iceland, is also a fishing center with a port. Here you can go on a whale watching adventure. Even though it is Iceland’s fourth largest city, it is possible to explore all the major spots in town by foot. Use of city bus transport is also free of charge. There are many natural wonders just outside the town that tourists can visit. They can go on a boat cruise and explore nearby islands, Hrisey and Grimsey. Akureyri also has one of Iceland's best skinning spots and the world's northeast botanic gardens. Some other attractions worth mentioning that you can do here are hiking, horse riding and bear bath.

Hofn

Hofn, is a town in southeastern part of Iceland. One of the attractions that makes this fisherman town unique is Jökulsárlón lagoon. Tourists can visit the lagoon and enjoy the amazing scenery painted by melting glaciers and unique black sand beaches. Just outside the town tourists can visit Iceland’s largest National Park Vatnajökull. There they will find many natural attractions worth visiting.

Egilsstadir

Located on the banks of river Lagarfljót, Egilsstadir is a town with a population of just over 2000 people. Similar Hofn this town is located just outside the National Park of Vatnajökull. Two main attractions that can be found near town are Iceland’s largest forest Hallormsstaðaskógur, and Lagarfjlót lake. Iceland’s largest forest covers about 740 hectares and is favored hiking place among tourist and local hikers. It is also home to many indigenous species to the area. Lake Lagarfjlót is famous for its legend about a beast called the Lagarfljót Wyrm that dates back to 1345.

Geysir

Geysir is a famous hot spring found in the geothermal area of Haukadalur. It is just a two hour drive from the main capital of Iceland, Reykjavik. Nowadays Geysir is rarely active, however visitors can still enjoy more smaller hot springs and geysers found in this area, such as Strokkur, Smiður and Litli-Strokkur.
